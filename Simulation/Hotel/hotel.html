Hotel Management System Features

Room Management

  • Add new rooms: Add new rooms to the hotel with details such as room number, type, capacity, and price.
  • Update room information: Update existing room information including availability status and amenities.
  • Remove rooms: Remove rooms that are no longer available for booking.

Reservation Management

  • Make reservations: Allow guests to make reservations for specific dates and room types.
  • Modify reservations: Allow guests to modify existing reservations including dates and room types.
  • Cancel reservations: Allow guests to cancel reservations if needed.

Check-in and Check-out

  • Check-in guests: Check guests in upon arrival and assign them to their reserved rooms.
  • Check-out guests: Check guests out when they leave and update room availability status.
  • Generate invoices: Generate invoices for checked-out guests including room charges and additional services.

Guest Management

  • Maintain guest records: Maintain records of guest information including names, contact details, and preferences.
  • Handle guest requests: Handle guest inquiries, requests, and complaints during their stay.
  • Provide concierge services: Offer additional services such as booking tours, transportation, or restaurant reservations.

Billing and Payment

  • Calculate charges: Calculate charges for room rates, additional services, and taxes.
  • Accept payments: Accept various payment methods including cash, credit cards, or vouchers.
  • Provide billing statements: Provide guests with itemized billing statements at check-out.

Reporting and Analytics

  • Generate reports: Generate reports on occupancy rates, revenue summaries, and guest demographics.
  • Analyze performance: Analyze hotel performance metrics such as RevPAR, ADR, and occupancy forecast.

User Authentication and Authorization

  • User authentication: Implement user authentication to ensure only authorized users can access sensitive functionalities.
  • Define user roles: Define user roles (e.g., admin, receptionist, housekeeper) with different levels of access permissions.

Error Handling

  • Handle errors gracefully: Handle errors gracefully and provide informative messages to guide users.
  • Validate user inputs: Validate user inputs to prevent invalid operations or data corruption.

Data Persistence

  • Store data: Use file handling or a database to store hotel information, room details, guest records, and transaction history.
  • Data integrity: Ensure data integrity and reliability to maintain accurate records over time.
